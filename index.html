Project Idea – Bilingual Robot and App
The project that involves IT that I would be most interested in is a combination of an application and something physical such as a robot or a toy that is able to teach children a second language.

The way I would envisage this is a robot that works together with a smart application that would be on an Apple or Android phone/tablet. This robot needs to be as acceptable to girls and boys, the widest age group possible and it would have to be cross-cultural. 

My motivation for such a program is that I have seen firsthand as a parent of bilingual children and as a teacher delivering lessons in English to non-native speakers. The world now really is becoming not only more “global,” but also especially in countries such as Australia, more multicultural. I feel strongly that any such technology which can be introduced at a young age would be highly beneficial.

Children always have and continue to play with toys. In this time, toys have evolved from sticks and stones to highly intelligent smart devices such as tablets, robotics, gaming consoles, and drones just to mention a few. In my experiences dealing with children, the children of today can adapt to technology much quicker in these last few years compared to only ten years ago. The technology that is available to them now is much more user-friendly and users are much more comfortable with it.
.

I would like to develop a robot similar to the Jimu Robot, or expand on the software for the Jimu Robot so that it would require the use of a different language to perform a task.

Ideally, this would be done via voice recognition selected on the app that controls the robot. The parent or teacher could set a task, for example, to have the robot navigate an obstacle course. Once the student could do this effectively in their mother tongue, the parent or teacher could change the settings and have the child redo the exercise in another chosen language.

There are already robots in toy stores that have preprogrammed responses to voice recognition however not as intelligent as Siri or Google. I want to develop one of these “loveable” robots or fluffy robotic toys to be able to interact with children using different languages. This could be a simple as replying to a Good morning gesture or even asking to please repeat the question in another specific language. 

Now all this sounds very simple, but this does involve some serious artificial intelligence. A group of experts would need to be assembled to decide on the outlines of the system. Experts such as teachers, linguists, child psychologists, protection agencies, translators, and others would need to develop the knowledge base. 

The Jimu robot as mentioned above used the Blockly coding system that is developed by Google. This coding system is very functional in the fact that the code can be exported to many of the popular coding languages such as Java Script, Python, PHP and others. Please see https://developers.google.com/blockly/guides/overview for more information. Therefore producing the actual code should not really be a major hurdle in developing this “bilingual robot”. 

Possible drawbacks would be that a voice recognition engineer would really need to make major changes to the “analog to digital systems”, as children’s voices are widely spread across the sound spectrum and their pronunciation is still developing. As this project involves language, there is also the difficulty of distinguishing accents of children originating from various countries. This is something I often see with my own children when they are using voice recognition to search up a youtube video and the results of their voice searches are often quite dissimilar to what they were trying to say. Another possible drawback is that the microphone would have to be small enough to fit into the robot, and the robot would most likely be in a noisy environment, therefore running a risk of receiving poorer audio quality making it more difficult yet again to digitize. 

By developing an intelligent application to be used on a device by the child would also be beneficial in a number of ways. By using words either written and/or spoken from the app would overcome a lot of the major voice recognition concerns. Also, the child would not only be able to improve their sight words and reading skills, but be able to learn written language that may not be in their current script such as roman numerals, sand scripts such as Tamil, Arabic or Thai and Asian languages such as, Hanzi and Kanji. 

Ultimately I feel that this technology would truly benefit the young learners of today and eventually make us all close “global citizens”. Its technology that is new, it is fun, it is educational and extremely useful for everyone.
